About CLG

Established in 2009, CLG works closely with the Joint Contracts Tribunal ("JCT") in representing Main Contractors' interests across all JCT contracts. CLG plays an instrumental role in ensuring that JCT's contracts are drafted such that the balance of risk imposed on Main Contractors is fair and equitable, and that such risks are passed down the supply chain where appropriate.

Membership with CLG presents the opportunity to be actively involved in implementation and revision of future JCT contracts and supports Continuing Professional Development for organisations involved in construction.

CLG leads and supports its members through change. It provides trusted professional services and offers relentless advocacy and upholds standards of excellence for its members.

Adjudicator Nomination Service

CLG offers an experienced adjudicator nomination service for resolving construction disputes in a simple and cost effective manner. Our Adjudicator Nomination Body ("ANB") is one of the four listed and approved ANB bodies named in JCT contracts as well as being named in other contracts such as DOM/1 and DOM/2 and the CIP Short Form of Sub-Contract.

National Federation of Builders

Founded in 1846, the NFB is the industry's longest established trade body. They represent builders, contractors, and house builders across England and Wales.

As one of the UK’s longest standing trade bodies, it was created to represent the building profession and to promote conditions its members need to thrive and contribute to the economic success of the UK.

Its members range from the sole trader to large, multi-million pound construction companies, with turnover ranging from below £500,000 to over £1.1 billion. The NFB provides expert advice, training, representation and business services for members so that they can comply with industry standards, excel in delivery, and grow successful businesses.

Scottish Building Federation

Established in 1895, the SBF has been a trusted advocate and representative for the construction industry in Scotland. Over the years, it has worked closely with its members, fostering a community that brings together ideals, addresses collective needs, and promotes collaborative construction business practices.

At SBF, they are dedicated to championing the interests and aspirations of their members across Scotland. Their membership base comprises hundreds of diverse companies, ranging from large corporations to medium-sized enterprises and small businesses, all actively engaged in construction and general contracting. This diverse network allows them to provide comprehensive support and tailored services to meet the unique requirements of their members.

National Access and Scaffolding Confederation

The NASC is a leading trade body in the UK for the access and scaffolding industry. Established in 1945, the NASC represents scaffolding companies, contracting firms, scaffolding manufacturers and businesses.

NASC offers unparalleled membership benefits to scaffolding industry businesses of all types and turnovers.

NASC contractor membership is also regarded by countless principal contractors, local authorities, utility providers and other organisations across the UK as a mark of quality, safety and professionalism.
